# Domain: Legal / Trademark Survey Evidence

The legal hat for surveys that serve as litigation/registry evidence. Pair with the methodological core. Follows the contract in `../../DOMAINS.md`. Highest stakes per survey: the instrument will be cross-examined, so every choice must be defensible and documented.

**Scope of this module:** survey *methodology* for evidentiary use. It is not legal advice. Admissibility turns on the jurisdiction and current case law and must be confirmed with counsel.

## 1. Scope
Consumer surveys offered as evidence: likelihood of confusion (infringement), secondary meaning / acquired distinctiveness, genericness, dilution/fame, descriptiveness, false advertising. Jurisdictions: US courts (and analogous Russian Rospatent / court-appointed sociological expertise, per past consumer trademark work).

## 2. Construct domains
likelihood_of_confusion, secondary_meaning, genericness, dilution_fame, descriptiveness, misleadingness, homogeneity_of_goods, interchangeability, element_dominance, well_known_status, reputational_harm, demographics.

## 3. Instruments, formats & sources
There is no public item bank here; the canon is **methodological formats** (free to use — methods, not copyright). See `references/survey-formats.md`:
- **Eveready** format — likelihood of confusion when the senior mark is strong/well known.
- **Squirt** format — confusion when the mark is less known; marks shown together; requires a control.
- **Teflon** format — genericness (brand vs. common name classification task).
- **Thermos** format — genericness via what consumers would ask for.
- Secondary-meaning and dilution designs.
ARA's own past instruments (incl. the Russian consumer trademark surveys) — proprietary_ARA.

## 4. Admissibility & expert layer
See `references/admissibility.md`. Core requirements: **define the relevant universe** correctly (prospective purchasers of the relevant goods/services — the most common ground for exclusion); **use a proper control** to net out noise/guessing; **double-blind administration** (neither interviewer nor respondent knows sponsor/purpose); **no leading questions**; **full documentation and chain of custody** because the expert may be deposed. US reliability framing: Daubert / FRE 702. Russian framing: two official sources apply — the Rospatent Guideline (Раздел IV, Гл. 2 «Проверка по основаниям для отказа») for the substantive construct tests, and the SIP Presidium information note **СП-21/15 (18.08.2022)** as the authoritative checklist for evaluating the survey (universe/sample/geography/wording/stimulus/subject/temporal — detailed in `references/admissibility.md`), alongside Rospatent Recommendations. Confirm specifics with counsel.

## 5. Benchmarks
Confusion/recognition thresholds discussed in case law and treatises (e.g., McCarthy) — as reference points, not fixed cutoffs; counsel interprets.

## 6. Sensitivities & population notes
The **universe definition** is the whole ballgame. Neutrality and non-advocacy are mandatory — the expert is not an advocate. For multilingual consumer markets, the 3MC lens applies to the universe and to language administration.

## 7. Defaults
Mode: often mall-intercept or controlled online with screening to the universe. Sampling: screened to the relevant consumer universe; **control questions/stimulus are always required**, while a separate control **cell/group** is the US default but case-specific in RF — do not apply the US control-cell default to RF cases automatically (see `references/survey-formats.md` § Jurisdiction note). For RF trademark work, size typically 400–2000 with socio-demographic + geographic spread across the RF (not one city/district) and randomness at the final stage; document the universe and selection method. Reporting: a defensible expert report with full methodology appendix.
