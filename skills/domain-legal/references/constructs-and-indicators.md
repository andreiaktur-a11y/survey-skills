# Trademark Constructs → Indicators → Thresholds (domain-specific)

Maps each trademark legal construct to (a) the question put to the expert, (b) standard empirical indicators (the survey questions that operationalize it), and (c) the threshold logic for concluding the construct is present or absent.

> **Provenance & IP.** Original synthesis grounded in two official (free-to-use) sources with different roles: the **Rospatent Guideline on trademark-registration administrative procedures** — specifically **Раздел IV, Глава 2 «Проверка по основаниям для отказа»**, which is the *substantive* authority defining each construct and its legal test (per Art. 1483 ГК РФ) — and the **SIP information note СП-21/15**, which governs how courts *evaluate the survey itself*. The construct→indicator→threshold approach and the standard, neutral question patterns are **standard practice** across court-submitted consumer surveys — method and legal-standard, not protected expression. Not copied from any single proprietary compilation. **Confirm construct definitions against the current Rospatent Guideline and exact threshold values against current Rospatent/SIP practice for the specific case.** Not legal advice.

All question patterns must obey `admissibility.md` (open-before-aided, control questions, no leading, neutral stimuli) and `../../../shared/question-quality.md`.

## How to use
For a given legal question, pick the construct, lift its indicator pattern, adapt wording to the actual mark/goods, sequence open→aided→control, set the threshold logic with counsel, and document everything in the report (`report-template.md`).

## Constructs

**Различительная способность / приобретённая (distinctiveness / secondary meaning).** Expert question: did the designation acquire distinctiveness (now or as of a date)? Indicators: unaided association of the designation with a single source; aided recognition; attribution to one vs. many producers. Threshold logic: presence shown by a high share associating it with one source; absence by dispersion across many/none.

**Сходство до степени смешения (likelihood of confusion).** Expert question: are the designations confusable for the average consumer of the relevant goods? Indicators: unaided — what producer/source does the disputed sign bring to mind; aided — perceived sameness of source/affiliation between the two signs; control comparison to net out baseline guessing. Use the triangulation approach (see `survey-formats.md` → правило треугольника). Threshold: net confusion (test minus control) above the case-appropriate level; mere surface similarity is not confusion.

**Введение в заблуждение (misleadingness).** Expert question: does the designation mislead as to producer, origin, quality, etc.? Indicators: what the sign communicates about origin/maker; whether that belief is false. Threshold: share holding the misleading belief.

**Вхождение во всеобщее употребление (genericness).** Expert question: has the designation become the common name for goods of that kind? Indicators (Teflon-style): after a calibrated brand-vs-common-name example, classify the term; (Thermos-style) what would you ask for to buy this product. Threshold: a majority treating it as a category name indicates genericness.

**Однородность товаров/услуг (homogeneity of goods/services).** Expert question: would consumers treat the goods/services as related/same-origin? Indicators: perceived likelihood that the goods come from the same/affiliated producer. Threshold: share perceiving common origin.

**Взаимозаменяемость (interchangeability).** Expert question: are the goods substitutes in the consumer's eyes? Indicators: would the consumer use one in place of the other; purchase substitution. Threshold: share treating them as interchangeable.

**Общеизвестность / широкая известность на дату (well-known status / fame as of a date).** Expert question: is the mark well known to the relevant consumers (as of the relevant date)? Indicators: unaided and aided recognition tied to the goods; temporal split «настоящее время / на дату в прошлом». Threshold: recognition level per Rospatent benchmarks, with adequate geographic spread across the RF (see `admissibility.md`).

**Доминирование элемента (element dominance).** Expert question: which element dominates perception of a composite designation? Indicators: what consumers notice/recall first; which element they use to name it. Threshold: share for whom the element dominates.

**Описательность (descriptiveness).** Expert question: is the designation perceived as describing the goods rather than indicating source? Indicators: spontaneous interpretation of the word; source vs. description. Threshold: share reading it as descriptive.

**Соответствие морали и общественным интересам; ущерб деловой репутации (morality / reputational harm).** Expert question: is the designation offensive, or does use harm reputation? Indicators: perceived offensiveness; effect on attitude toward the producer. Threshold: case-specific share.
