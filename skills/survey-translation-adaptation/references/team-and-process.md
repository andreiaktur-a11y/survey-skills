# Team & Process

Who does the work and in what order, so a multilingual instrument is defensible and reproducible. Pairs with `adaptation-vs-translation.md` (the TRAPD method) and `../../../shared/3mc-considerations.md`.

## Roles
- **Translators (≥2 per language):** native target-language, fluent source, familiar with survey language and the subject domain. Independent first drafts.
- **Reviewer / methodologist:** convenes the reconciliation, checks construct fidelity and scale behaviour, not just grammar.
- **Adjudicator:** makes the final call on each contested item; owns the final version.
- **Cognitive interviewer:** runs pretests with target-language respondents.
- Keep translators independent of whoever wrote the source items where possible (fresh reading).

## Workflow
1. **Prepare** — translation brief: construct definitions, intended meaning per item, scale intent, terms to keep constant, do-not-literalize flags.
2. **Parallel translation** — independent drafts per language.
3. **Reconciliation review** — translators + methodologist compare, resolve divergences, flag equivalence risks.
4. **Adjudication** — adjudicator fixes the version.
5. **Pretest** — cognitive interviews (aim ~5–9 per language); probe comprehension, retrieval, and scale use.
6. **Revise & document** — finalize; record every decision, alternative, and rationale.
7. **Version & store** — label per-language `validation_status` honestly in `shared/instrument-library/` (`adapted`/`draft` until validated); date it.

## Documentation (the deliverable behind the deliverable)
For each item and language: chosen wording, alternatives considered, adjudication rationale, pretest findings, residual equivalence caveats. This audit trail is what makes the instrument defensible and lets the next wave reuse decisions instead of relitigating them.

## Fast-track (small internal jobs)
Even when full TRAPD isn't affordable, never skip: (a) a second independent reviewer, (b) at least light cognitive pretesting per language, (c) documentation. Mark the result `draft`/`adapted` and say what was skipped — don't present it as validated.
