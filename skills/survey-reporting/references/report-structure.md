# Report Structure

General skeleton for a research report/evidence package. **Legal заключения use the dedicated `../../domain-legal/references/report-template.md`** (defensibility-first, signature, appendix) — this file is for CMA/HR/marketing deliverables. Write findings in plain language; put method depth in the appendix, not the body.

## Sections
1. **Executive summary** — the decisions the reader can make from this, in ≤1 page: what was measured, headline results with intervals, and the main caveat. Written last, read first.
2. **Objectives** — the question the study answers and why; scope and out-of-scope.
3. **Method** — mode, instrument (reused validated items + provenance), fielding window; enough for a reader to judge fitness, detail to the appendix.
4. **Sample & coverage** — universe, achieved n, weighting, and **per-group coverage** (for multilingual/CMA, each language group's n and reach — coverage = credibility).
5. **Results** — organized by objective, not by question order. Every estimate with its interval; charts show uncertainty; group comparisons only where the equivalence gate passed (`../../survey-analysis/references/measurement-equivalence.md`).
6. **Limitations** — named honestly, with direction of any bias. Absent limitations sections read as overclaiming.
7. **Appendix** — instrument, scales, full tables, weighting detail, exclusions, provenance of reused items.

## Domain notes
- **CMA / QMP evidence package** — foreground per-composite top-box by language group and coverage evidence; state the monitoring period; **no PHI / no identifiers** anywhere in the report or appendix.
- **Marketing/social** — lead with the decision implication; keep method in appendix.
- **Every report** — reused-item attribution carried through; no verbatim tier-C content; conclusions stay within what the data support.
